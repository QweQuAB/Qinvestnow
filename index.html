Key investing terms
Stock / Share
A tiny piece of ownership in a company. If Apple has 1 billion shares and you own 1, you literally own one-billionth of Apple.
ETF (Exchange-Traded Fund)
A basket of many stocks in one. QQQ holds 100 companies — one purchase gives you instant diversification. Perfect for beginners.
Dividend
A portion of a company's profit paid directly to you. Coca-Cola pays ~3% per year. Realty Income pays every single month.
Compound Interest
When your returns earn more returns. $138 at 10%/year for 8 years more than doubles — because earnings keep reinvesting automatically.
Dollar-Cost Averaging
Investing a fixed amount monthly regardless of price. When prices drop you automatically buy more cheaply. Removes the stress of timing the market.
ROI (Return on Investment)
ROI = (Net Income ÷ Cost of Investment) × 100. If you invest GH₵ 1,500 and earn GH₵ 300 after a year, your ROI = 20%.
50/30/20 Budget Rule
A simple budgeting guide: spend 50% of income on needs, 30% on wants, and save/invest at least 20%. Your budget tab helps you track this.
Emergency Fund
3–6 months of living expenses saved somewhere liquid (easy to access). This must be built before you invest heavily. It is your financial safety net.
Net Income
Total income minus all taxes, savings and expenses. If you earn GH₵ 3,000, save GH₵ 300, and spend GH₵ 2,400, your net = GH₵ 300. A positive net means you're living within your means.
The golden rules
1
Budget first, invest second. Use the Budget tab to find how much you can genuinely afford to invest monthly without stress.
2
Never invest money you need soon. Stocks are for 3+ years minimum. Keep 3–6 months expenses in Cash Savings first.
3
Consistency beats timing. Investing GH₵ 200 every month beats trying to pick the "perfect" moment to invest a lump sum.
4
Aim for 20% saved/invested. Try to save or invest at least 20% of your monthly income. Your Budget tab tracks this automatically.
